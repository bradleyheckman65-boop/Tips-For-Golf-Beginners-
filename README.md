<p>
Golf equipment plays an important role in how well a player performs on the course. Different clubs are designed for specific situations during a round of golf. The driver is typically used for long distance shots from the tee box. Irons are more versatile and can be used for a variety of approach shots. Wedges are designed for short shots around the green. Finally, the putter is used to roll the ball into the hole on the putting green. Choosing the right club for each shot requires experience and understanding of distance. Golfers often carry a full set of clubs to prepare for different situations. Using the correct equipment can improve accuracy and consistency.
</p>

<p>
In addition to clubs, golfers also use other equipment such as golf balls, tees, and gloves. Golf balls come in different designs that affect spin and distance. Beginners often choose balls designed for forgiveness and control. Gloves help improve grip and reduce slipping during swings. Comfortable golf shoes provide traction and stability while walking the course. Many golfers also use range finders to measure distance to the hole. Having the right equipment can make the game more enjoyable and easier to learn. As players gain experience, they often upgrade their gear to match their playing style. Understanding golf equipment helps golfers make better decisions during each round.
</p>
